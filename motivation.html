Akash Mudra

Akash Mudra also known as the "Space Mudra" or "Aakash Mudra," is a hand gesture or mudra used in yoga and meditation practices. It is believed to help balance the element of space (akash) within the body and promote a sense of calmness, openness, and expansiveness.

To perform Akash Mudra:

  • Start by sitting in a comfortable meditation posture, such as cross-legged on the floor or in a chair with your spine straight.
  • Relax your hands on your knees or thighs.
  • Bring your arms in front of your chest, keeping your elbows bent and close to your body.
  • Join the tips of your middle finger and thumb, creating a circle or ring shape. The other fingers should be extended and pointing upward.
  • Keep your wrists relaxed and your hands comfortably resting in front of your chest.
  • Take slow, deep breaths and allow yourself to feel a sense of spaciousness and openness.
  • Hold the mudra for several minutes or as long as feels comfortable and beneficial.

Benefits :

Expands consciousness, improves intuition, enhances mental clarity.

Release:

  • Gently separate your middle finger and thumb, releasing the circle or ring shape created by joining their tips.
  • Allow your fingers to naturally unfold and extend.
  • Relax your hands on your knees or thighs.
  • letting them rest in a comfortable position.
  • Take a moment to observe any sensations or shifts in your energy after releasing the mudra.
  • If you wish, you can take a few deep breaths or continue with your meditation or yoga practice.

Duration :

  • Practice for 10-15 minutes daily.
  • You can extend it to 40-45 minutes Daily.

Brahma Mudra

Brahma Mudra is a hand gesture or "mudra" used in various yogic and meditation practices. In Sanskrit, "Brahma" refers to the creator deity in Hinduism, and "mudra" means a symbolic gesture or seal. Brahma Mudra is said to stimulate and balance the energy in the body, particularly in the upper chakras.

To perform Brahma Mudra:

  • Sit in a comfortable position, such as cross-legged on the floor or on a chair with your spine upright.
  • Relax your shoulders and close your eyes, taking a few deep breaths to center yourself.
  • Bring your hands up to your chest level, with the palms facing outward and the fingers pointing upwards.
  • Bend your elbows and bring your fingertips together, creating a shape similar to a lotus flower.
  • Gently press the fingertips of both hands together, applying light pressure.
  • Rest your hands in this position on your chest, near the center of your sternum.
  • Maintain a relaxed and focused state as you hold the mudra.
  • You can practice Brahma Mudra for a few minutes to several minutes, depending on your comfort level and the duration of your meditation or yoga session.

Benefits :

Enhances spiritual connection, promotes a sense of surrender and devotion.

Release:

  • Take a moment to become aware of your body and breath. Slowly and gently release the pressure between your fingertips, allowing your hands to separate.
  • Relax your arms and let them rest comfortably by your sides or on your lap.
  • If your eyes were closed, you can open them and reorient yourself to your surroundings.
  • Take a few deep breaths, allowing yourself to transition from the mudra to a state of relaxation.
  • You can continue with your meditation or yoga practice, or simply go about your daily activities.

Duration :

  • Practice for 10-15 minutes daily.
  • You can extend it to 40-45 minutes Daily.

Garuda Mudra

Garuda Mudra also known as Eagle Mudra, is a symbolic hand gesture or seal used in certain forms of yoga and meditation practices. Mudras are gestures or positions that are believed to direct the flow of energy within the body and enhance spiritual or mental states

To perform Garuda Mudra:

  • Begin by sitting in a comfortable meditation posture, such as cross-legged on the floor or in a chair with your spine erect.
  • Extend your arms in front of you at shoulder level, parallel to the ground. Keep your elbows slightly bent.
  • Cross your arms at the elbow, with your right arm on top. Bring your palms together, fingers pointing upward.
  • Interlock your fingers, with your left thumb pressing against the right thumb.
  • Bend your elbows to bring your hands closer to your chest. Your forearms should be perpendicular to the ground, creating a shape resembling wings.
  • Relax your shoulders and breathe deeply, focusing on the energy flowing through your hands and arms.
  • Maintain a relaxed and focused state as you hold the mudra.
  • Hold the mudra for a few minutes or as long as feels comfortable and beneficial.

Benefits :

Balances energy, promotes focus and concentration, relieves tension in the shoulders.Stress and Anxiety too.

Release:

  • While maintaining the crossed arms position, slowly lower your hands down towards your lap or thighs.
  • Gently separate your palms and fingers, letting your hands rest comfortably on your legs.
  • Take a few deep breaths and allow your body and mind to relax.
  • Take a few deep breaths, allowing yourself to transition from the mudra to a state of relaxation.
  • You can shake out your hands or rotate your wrists gently to release any residual tension.

Duration :

  • Practice for 5-10 minutes daily.
  • You can extend it to 30-35 minutes Daily.

Kumber Mudra

Kumber Mudra refers to a hand gesture or pose in yoga and meditation practices. It is named after Kuber, the Hindu deity associated with wealth and abundance. The Kuber mudra is believed to help stimulate the flow of energy related to material prosperity and financial stability.

To perform Kumber Mudra:

  • Sit in a comfortable meditation posture, such as cross-legged on the floor or on a chair with your spine straight.
  • Relax your whole body and take a few deep breaths to center yourself.
  • Touch the tips of your thumb, index finger, and middle finger together, while keeping the other fingers extended.
  • Maintain a gentle pressure between the fingers, but not too tight.
  • Rest your hands on your thighs with the palms facing upward.
  • Close your eyes and focus your attention on your breath or any positive intentions related to wealth and abundance.
  • Maintain a relaxed and focused state as you hold the mudra.
  • Hold the mudra for as long as you feel comfortable, ideally for at least 5 to 15 minutes.

Benefits :

Enhances focus and concentration, promotes mental clarity, stimulates brain activity.

Release:

  • Slowly release the interlaced fingers and let your hands relax naturally.
  • You can shake out your hands or gently roll your wrists to release any tension.
  • Take a moment to observe any changes or sensations you may feel in your body and mind.
  • Take a few deep breaths, allowing yourself to transition from the mudra to a state of relaxation.
  • Breathe deeply and notice how the practice has affected you.

Duration :

  • Beginner's Duration: Start with 2-3 minutes of practice and gradually increase the time as you become more comfortable and confident in the mudra.
  • Standard Duration: The recommended duration for practicing the Kubera Mudra is 10-15 minutes daily. This duration allows you to experience the benefits of the mudra while being manageable within a daily routine.

Prithvi Mudra

Prithvi Mudra also known as Earth Mudra or Mudra of Earth, is a hand gesture or mudra that is believed to stimulate the element of earth within the body. It is thought to promote stability, grounding, and nourishment.

To perform Kumber Mudra:

  • Sit comfortably in a meditation posture, with your spine straight.\n->Bring your hands in front of your body at the level of your lower abdomen.
  • Touch the tip of your ring finger (also known as the "earth" finger) to the tip of your thumb.
  • Extend the other three fingers (index, middle, and pinky finger) comfortably.
  • Rest your hands on your thighs or in your lap.
  • Close your eyes, relax your body, and take slow, deep breaths.
  • Close your eyes and focus your attention on your breath or any positive intentions related to wealth and abundance.
  • Allow your awareness to focus on the mudra and visualize a sense of stability and grounding.
  • Hold the mudra for several minutes or as long as feels comfortable and beneficial.

Benefits :

Grounds the body and mind, improves stability, promotes a sense of security.

Release:

  • To release Prithvi Mudra, simply separate the ring finger from the thumb. Allow your fingers to naturally relax and return to a comfortable position.
  • You can rest your hands on your thighs or in your lap.
  • As you release the mudra, take a moment to observe any sensations or shifts in your energy.
  • Notice the effects the mudra had on your body, mind, and emotions.

Duration :

  • Beginner's Duration: Start with 3-5 minutes of practice and gradually increase the time as you become more comfortable and confident in the mudra.
  • Standard Duration: The recommended duration for practicing the Prithvi Mudra is 15-20 minutes daily.
